  • Aspen Point Campground @ Lake of the Woods

    Located approximately 40 minutes west of Klamath Falls Oregon, Aspen Point Campground offers many amenities.  This scenic campground is located on the northeast shore of Lake of the Woods. It features 58 camp sites with a camp host.  Within the total campsites, 50 are RV sites, eight double campsites, seven tent only campsites, and one group site. 

    There are five restrooms (maintained May 1st though October 1st).  Improvements at Aspen Point campground include durable aggregate around every picnic table and fire grate to diminish overall dust and dirt.

    Visitors can enjoy motorized and non-motorized boating activities, fishing, bike riding, swimming, as well as the nearby amenities of the Lake of the Woods resort.

    Aspen Point Campground has an adjacent Day Use Area for picknicking and accessing the High Lakes Trail which leads to Fish Lake.  Boat launch facilities, docks, and parking are available between the day use area and campground

  • Sunset Campground @ Lake of the Woods

    Located 40 minutes west of Klamath Falls, Oregon, Sunset Campground provides 64 camp sites and a camp host. These campsites include picnic tables, and fire rings. All 64 camp sites provide RV parking (although there are no hook-ups available) and two of the campsites are ADA accessible.

    There are three renovated restrooms, one for each loop, with electric flush toilets, running water, and automatic hand dryers. Nearby, the Lake of the Woods Resort offers dining options, boat rentals, and basic supplies.

    Sunset also has a day use area, tucked in next to the campground and ideal for boating. Besides its  picnic tables, boat ramp, and boat trailer parking spaces, there is a compact, gravelly beach perfect for swimming. The day use area is a great spot for campers and non-camping friends to meet up for a day of water recreation on Lake of the Woods. Visitors can enjoy both motorized and non-motorized boating activities, fishing, bike riding, and swimming.

  • Slide Lake Trail

    This trail accesses Slide Lake for fishing and picnicking opportunities. It is 1.0 miles long, and begins at the Slide Lake Trailhead on Forest Road 3360. The trail follows an abandoned road for most of its length. Some views of the geologically interesting collapsed face of Slide Mountain are offered from this trail.

    Once at Slide Lake, you can relax, fish, birdwatch, and/or picnic in a lush mixed conifer setting. White fir, incense cedar, ponderosa and lodgepole pine trees offer shade and tranquility. There are also some dispersed camping sites at the lake, making this a good beginner backpacking destination.
